## Wake Word Feature

Wake word detection allows you to activate SlasshyWispr's assistant mode using a voice phrase instead of pressing a hotkey. Simply say your wake phrase (like "Hey Lily") to start a conversation with your voice assistant.

This feature enables:
- Hands-free assistant activation
- Natural conversation flow
- Quick access while typing or working
- Always-listening assistant mode when enabled

<Note>
  Wake word detection is part of SlasshyWispr's command mode and assistant features. When enabled, the app listens for your wake phrase in the background.
</Note>

## Configuration Settings

Wake word functionality is controlled by two key settings:

### Wake Word Enabled

**Setting**: `wakeWordEnabled` (boolean)

**Default**: `false` (disabled)

Enables or disables wake word detection. When turned on, SlasshyWispr listens for your wake phrase and automatically enters assistant mode when detected.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open General Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings > General** in SlasshyWispr.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable Wake Word">
    Toggle the **Wake Word Enabled** option to turn on wake word detection.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test Activation">
    Say your wake phrase (e.g., "Hey Lily") to test the activation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Assistant Name

**Setting**: `assistantName` (string)

**Default**: `"Lily"`

The name your assistant responds to. This forms the wake phrase pattern (e.g., "Hey Lily", "OK Lily").

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a Name">
    Pick a distinctive name that's easy to pronounce and unlikely to trigger accidentally. The default is "Lily".
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Update Assistant Name">
    In **Settings > General**, change the **Assistant Name** field to your preferred name.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use Natural Phrases">
    Your wake phrase typically follows patterns like:
    - "Hey [Name]"
    - "OK [Name]"
    - "[Name]"
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How Wake Word Detection Works

When wake word detection is enabled, SlasshyWispr:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Background Listening">
    Continuously listens to your microphone in the background for the wake phrase.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Wake Phrase Recognition">
    When it detects a phrase matching your wake word pattern ("Hey [AssistantName]"), it activates.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assistant Mode Activation">
    Automatically switches to assistant mode and begins recording your command or question.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Processing & Response">
    Processes your speech, generates an AI response, and speaks the answer using TTS (text-to-speech).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Wake word detection requires continuous microphone access. Ensure your microphone is properly configured in **Settings > General** and that SlasshyWispr has necessary permissions.
</Warning>

## Customizing Wake Phrases

While SlasshyWispr uses your configured `assistantName`, you can optimize your wake phrase experience:

### Choosing an Effective Name

<Accordion title="Use Distinctive Names">
  Choose names with 2-3 syllables that are phonetically distinct. Names like "Lily", "Jarvis", "Atlas" work well.
  
  **Good examples**: Lily, Jarvis, Atlas, Nova, Echo
  
  **Avoid**: Common words, single syllables, names that sound like common phrases
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Avoid Common Words">
  Don't use names that sound like words you frequently say ("Hey", "OK", common names of people around you).
  
  This reduces false activations during normal conversation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Test in Your Environment">
  Test your chosen name in your actual working environment. Say it in different tones and volumes to ensure consistent detection.
</Accordion>

### Wake Phrase Patterns

SlasshyWispr recognizes wake phrases that include your assistant name. Common patterns:

- **"Hey [Name]"**: Most natural and commonly used (e.g., "Hey Lily")
- **"OK [Name]"**: Alternative activation phrase (e.g., "OK Lily")
- **"[Name]"**: Direct name call may work depending on implementation

<Tip>
  Speak clearly and at normal volume. Shouting or whispering may reduce detection accuracy.
</Tip>

## Tips for Reliable Activation

### Microphone Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Quality Microphone">
    Use a good quality microphone for best detection. USB microphones or headset mics typically work better than built-in laptop mics.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check Microphone Settings">
    In **Settings > General**, verify the correct microphone device is selected under **Microphone Device**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test Audio Levels">
    Ensure your microphone picks up your voice clearly without distortion or excessive background noise.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Environment Optimization

<Note>
  **Reduce Background Noise**: Wake word detection works best in quiet environments. Reduce background music, conversations, or ambient noise when possible.
</Note>

<Accordion title="Optimal Speaking Distance">
  Maintain a consistent distance from your microphone. For desktop mics, 6-12 inches typically works well.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Clear Pronunciation">
  Speak the wake phrase clearly and naturally. Don't rush or mumble the assistant name.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pause After Wake Word">
  After saying the wake phrase, pause briefly (about 1 second) before stating your command or question. This gives the system time to fully activate.
</Accordion>

### Troubleshooting Detection Issues

<Accordion title="False Activations">
  **Problem**: Assistant activates when you don't want it to.
  
  **Solutions**:
  - Choose a more distinctive assistant name
  - Reduce microphone sensitivity if your OS/hardware allows
  - Disable wake word when not needed
  - Check for background audio (TV, music) that might contain similar sounds
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Missed Activations">
  **Problem**: Wake word doesn't trigger when you say it.
  
  **Solutions**:
  - Speak louder and more clearly
  - Check microphone is selected correctly in settings
  - Test microphone in system settings to ensure it's working
  - Verify wake word is enabled (`wakeWordEnabled: true`)
  - Try different wake phrase patterns ("Hey Lily" vs "OK Lily")
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Delayed Response">
  **Problem**: Long delay between wake word and activation.
  
  **Solutions**:
  - Check CPU usage - background processes may slow detection
  - Ensure local STT models are warmed up if using offline mode
  - Close unnecessary applications to free system resources
</Accordion>

## Command Mode vs Wake Word

SlasshyWispr offers multiple ways to activate assistant mode:

| Method | Activation | Use Case |
|--------|------------|----------|
| **Wake Word** | Voice phrase ("Hey Lily") | Hands-free, always available |
| **Command Hotkey** | Keyboard shortcut (default: `Ctrl+Shift+Space`) | Quick activation while typing |
| **Push-to-Talk** | Hold hotkey (default: `Ctrl+Space`) | Precise control of recording |

<Tip>
  You can use wake word and hotkeys together! Enable wake word for hands-free access, but still use hotkeys when you prefer keyboard control.
</Tip>

### When to Use Wake Word

- During hands-free work (cooking, exercising, away from keyboard)
- While typing or using the mouse
- For quick queries without interrupting workflow
- When you want natural, conversational assistant access

### When to Use Hotkeys Instead

- In noisy environments where wake word detection is unreliable
- When you need precise control over recording start/stop
- During meetings or calls (to avoid accidental activation)
- When wake word false positives are problematic

## Privacy Considerations

<Warning>
  **Microphone Access**: When wake word detection is enabled, SlasshyWispr requires continuous access to your microphone to listen for the wake phrase. 
  
  If you're concerned about always-on listening:
  - Use hotkeys instead of wake word
  - Disable wake word when not needed
  - Check that you're using local STT models if you want processing to stay on-device
</Warning>

<Note>
  **Local Processing**: If you're using local STT models (offline mode), wake word detection happens entirely on your device with no data sent to external servers.
</Note>

## Example Configuration

### Full Wake Word Setup

```json
{
  "wakeWordEnabled": true,
  "assistantName": "Lily",
  "commandMode": true,
  "microphoneDeviceId": "default",
  "sttRuntimeMode": "local",
  "localSttModel": "nvidia/parakeet-tdt-0.6b-v3"
}
```

**This configuration**:
- Enables wake word detection
- Sets assistant name to "Lily" (say "Hey Lily" to activate)
- Uses local STT for privacy
- Keeps command mode enabled for assistant features

### Alternative: Hotkey-Only Setup

```json
{
  "wakeWordEnabled": false,
  "commandHotkey": "Ctrl+Shift+Space",
  "pushToTalkHotkey": "Ctrl+Space"
}
```

**This configuration**:
- Disables wake word
- Uses keyboard shortcuts only
- No continuous microphone listening

<Tip>
  Start with wake word disabled, learn the hotkeys, then enable wake word once you're comfortable with the assistant features.
</Tip>
